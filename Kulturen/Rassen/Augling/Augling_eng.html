Description

Eyelings have an oval, almost round body with one strong leg that allows high jumping and an average height of about a meter. They have claw-like, webbed feet and one enormous, reptile-like centered eye, hence the name. A giant cangaroo tail that is placed on the back of their bodies hangs down to the ground and assures the Eyelings stability and balance.

Behaviour

Eyelings need water in order to survive and not suffer from dehydration of their eye. They can breath underwater but their swimming and diving skills are quite primitive.
The Eyeling's eye produces sticky secretion as a method to prevent dust and dirt from harming or polluting the eye. The notable sticky property makes it a great and widely-used glue. Eyelings possess certain psychic powers. Since their lack of speaking organs in the general sense, their exclusive mean of communication is telepathy through eye contact. They are also able to communicate with small animals and move around small objects with telekinesis.
Many Eyelings work as engineers.

Food

Eyelings don't need to sleep but rather "fill up" their energy level by absorbing and filtering water through their skin which is necessary for their survival.

Habitat/Origin

The continent of Wirinima is the homestead of Eyelings. They mainly live around lakes and rivers.

Reproduction

Eyelings are hermaphrodites. To reproduce, two individuals face each other and stare intensively. Both imagine the potential offspring and if the images match each other, implying a special connection, an egg is produced through the telepathic energy building up on the central point in the air between the two Eyelings. This egg needs about a mooncycle to hatch.
